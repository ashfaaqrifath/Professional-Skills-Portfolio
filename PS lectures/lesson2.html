Lesson 02Portfolio management

Portfolios are used for many purposes. In job applications, they show work samples and skills to employers. They also help with career advancement, showcasing achievements for promotion or new opportunities. In the creative industries, artists, writers, and designers use portfolios to showcase their work and style to clients. Students use portfolios to track their progress and demonstrate learning achievements. For educational admissions, portfolios help showcase extracurricular activities and skills. Teachers use them to demonstrate their teaching effectiveness and growth. Freelancers and consultants use portfolios to attract clients by showing previous projects and testimonials.

Portfolios also provide professional development, certifications, workshops, and showcase new skills. They can help with performance reviews at work and showcase specific skills, such as coding or leadership. Entrepreneurs can use portfolios to present business plans to investors. They can also help with networking, building a personal brand, and highlighting skills when changing careers.

There are different types of portfolios: personal, professional, employment, and assessment portfolios. Online platforms like LinkedIn, Behance, Google Sites, and WordPress help create portfolios. These sites are easy to use and allow people to showcase their work and skills to others.
